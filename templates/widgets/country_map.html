This map represents the health status of children under 5 years of age by state, based on a combination of factors: immunization rates (DPT, measles), skilled birth attendance, postnatal visitation, bednet usage, child mortality, and access to appropriate treatment for severe dehydration and malaria.

Data comes from the DHS survey 2008.

This map represents an overview of child nutrition by state, based on the proportion of children in each state who are underweight, wasted, and/or stunted.

Data comes from the DHS survey 2008.

This map represents the status of malaria by state, as measured by the proportion of households that have at least one insecticide-treated bed net, the proportion of children under 5 who sleep under insecticide-treated bed nets, and proportion of children under 5 with access to appropriate anti-malaria treatment.

Data comes from the DHS survey 2008.

This map represents the status of maternal health services by state, including antenatal care, skilled birth attendance, postnatal visitation, and HIV testing during pregnancy.

Data comes from the DHS survey 2008.
